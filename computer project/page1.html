National Parks of India

National Park refers to an area that is reserved for the protection and betterment of the wildlife and biodiversity and the activities like poaching, hunting, development, grazing, etc., are not permitted. Besides this, its borders are well marked and distinct.

At present as of November 2023, there are 106 national parks in India. It has the third-highest number of national parks in Asia after China and Thailand. The list of top 30 national parks in India is given below including their characteristic features.there are top 10 national parks listed below:

1. Jim Corbett National Park

Location: Uttarakhand
About: It is situated in the district of Nainital in the state of Uttarakhand, India and is also a part of Corbett Tiger Reserve. Jim Corbett is a perfect place for wildlife enthusiasts. It got its name from Jim Corbett that was a hunter who later became a conservationist.

Area: Jim Corbett National Park is the oldest national park in India spread over 500 square kilometres
Established: 1936
Flora: The flora of this place comprises tall grasslands, thick Sal forests, winding streams, etc.
Fauna:It is home to royal Bengal tiger and various other types of wildlife such as leopard, buffalo, deer, sloth bears, jackals, elephants, etc. It is also home to around 600 species of birds

2.kaziranga national park

Location: Assam
About:Kaziranga National Park is located in the north-eastern region of India in the Nagaon and Golaghat districts of Assam.Kaziranga National Park accorded with the status of UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1985 and in 2006 it was also declared a tiger reserve by the govt. of India under its 'Tiger Project.'The park offers Jeep Safari and Elephant safari for the tourist to allow them to see the wildlife closely in their natural habitat. The best time to visit this place is between November and April. It may be closed during monsoon due to safety reasons.

Area:It is spread over 430 sq. km and is famous for being home to the largest population of endangered one-horned rhinoceros
Established: 1947
Flora:It has a rich variety of plants, trees and wild animals. Its flora comprises grasslands, evergreen forests, and tropical semi-evergreen forests. Besides this, it also has water lilies, lotus, and water hyacinth and Rattan Cane (climbing palm).
Fauna: It is home to over 30 mammalian species out of which 15 are in the threatened category. The popular species of reptiles in the park include Snakes, Tortoises, Alligator, Crocodile, etc.
